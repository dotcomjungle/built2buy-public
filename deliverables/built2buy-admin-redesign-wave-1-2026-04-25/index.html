Built2Buy · Admin Redesign · Wave 1

Stakeholder & Functional Map

v2 Draft · April 25, 2026 · Authored by Seven for Steve Rice

A map-stage design for the new Built2Buy administration panel. The current admin is organized around plugins (~50 of them, sprawling). The new admin will be organized around the people who do the work and the work they do. This document defines who those people are, what work they own, and how they share the system.

v2 Draft · iterating · not yet a build-ready engineering brief
Foundation Stakeholders Functional Areas Access Matrix
Foundation Principle
Built2Buy defines the lanes because we know how businesses work. Customers choose which roles they need; the role definitions themselves are platform-controlled. The strength we bring is keeping people in their lane — preventing one stakeholder from working inside someone else's job.
— Steve Rice, April 25, 2026
01

Stakeholder Map

Eleven stakeholder roles across two stacks. The Built2Buy super-admin is the platform side — that's us. The customer stakeholders are the ten roles inside our customer organizations (the wholesale distribution companies who use Built2Buy).

Built2Buy Super-Admin

Platform-side · 1 role
Platform Operator
v1 name: Built2Buy Super-Admin
Built2Buy staff. Runs the multi-tenant platform across all customer instances. Provisions sites, deploys updates, sees telemetry across the fleet, owns integrations.
When a customer reports a bug, reproduce it without disrupting their site, so I can fix before they call back.
Real at: Built2Buy itself · Never present at any customer site.

Customer Stakeholders

Customer-side · 10 roles
Owner / Administrator
The customer's top admin
Final authority on margin, pricing policy, role assignments. Mostly logs in for dashboards and approvals, not daily admin work.
Open the system Monday morning, know in 30 seconds if the business is healthy.
Real at: Every customer.
Sales Director
Top of the sales hierarchy
Sees and acts on all sales activity. Manages Regional Sales Managers. Sets sales policy and targets.
Know how the sales team is performing this week, by region and by rep.
Real at: Mid-to-large customers (~$10M+) · At smaller customers, this is the Owner with a hat on.
Regional Sales Manager
also: Internal Sales Rep
Manages reps in their region. Sees their region's accounts and orders. Doubles as customer support for those accounts.
When a rep is underperforming, see what's happening with their accounts so I can coach or reassign.
Real at: Mid-to-large customers.
Phone Team
Desk module
Desk-based order entry for ALL accounts. Doubles as customer support — handles "where's my order?", returns, balance questions. Does not configure anything.
Place a buyer's order in under two minutes while they're on the phone.
Real at: All customers above ~$2M revenue.
Field Sales Rep
also: Outside Sales Rep
Sees only their assigned accounts. Places orders for those. Doubles as customer support for them. Often mobile — at a buyer's site or in transit.
When in front of a buyer, place their order on the spot without looking like an IT person.
Real at: All customers · Mobile-first context where present.
Marketing Coordinator
v1 name: Marketing Manager
Site content, banners, email blasts, promotions, GA. Owns coupons but NOT list pricing. Often part-time or outsourced at small customers.
When a promotion runs, get it onto the site and to buyers in one motion.
Real at: $5M+ customers · Below that, this is the Owner or a contractor.
Catalog Manager
also: Buyer / Category Manager
Loads and maintains products, attributes, images, and cost. Read-only on sell price (Owner and Sales Director set sell price; Catalog Manager loads cost).
When new products arrive, add them in bulk without typing every field.
Real at: $3M+ customers · Often the most spreadsheet-fluent person in the building.
Order Processor
Office side
Office-based. Processes incoming orders end-to-end through invoicing. Handles order edits, customer-comms about order status. Does not configure shipping rules.
Process orders to shipped without re-keying anything.
Real at: All customers · At small customers may be the Phone Team.
Shipping & Warehouse Lead
Floor side · includes warehouse picking
Configures shipping rules, runs the warehouse floor, picks/packs/labels. Owns the shipping rate aggregator. Tablet-friendly UI needed.
Get today's orders out the door correctly, with the right rate and the right label.
Real at: All customers · Owner does this at the smallest customers.
Bookkeeper / Finance
May be in-house OR external accounting firm
AR/AP, QuickBooks sync via Webgility, tax reports, invoice and payment records. Read-heavy on orders, write on financial adjustments.
Reconcile the month without surprises.
Real at: All customers · External firms need their own auditable login.

Functional Area Map

The other axis. Ten functional areas, dual-named: a noun (what it is) and a verb (what gets done). The final UI may use one or the other; we keep both during the design phase. Areas are platform-defined; customers enable subsets per their needs but cannot redefine them.

01
Orders & Fulfillment
Process orders
Order entry, editing, processing through to invoicing. Customer-comms about order status.
What lives here: WC Orders, dcj-admin-order-entry, PDF Invoices, Webgility order sync, checkout field editor, customer addresses on orders.
02
Shipping
Configure shipping
Carrier setup, rate rules, zones, methods. Single rate aggregator resolving the 5-plugin overlap into one canonical "what rate fires" view.
What lives here: WC Shipping Zones, table-rate-shipping, dcj-ups-live-rates, dcj-collect-shipping-option, Wholesale Suite shipping logic.
03
Pricing & Promotions
Set prices and promotions
List pricing, wholesale tiers, customer-specific contract pricing, coupons, payment terms. Owner has final margin authority.
What lives here: WC product pricing, wholesale-prices, wholesale-prices-premium, auto-added-coupons-pro, dcj-terms-gateway, WC Coupons.
04
Accounts
Manage customers and leads
Buyer directory, lead approval workflow, role assignment, contact records, account notes.
What lives here: WC Customers, WP Users, woocommerce-wholesale-lead-capture, users-customers-import-export, user-switching, wholesale role assignment.
05
The Catalog
Maintain the catalog
Products, attributes, categories, galleries, filters. Cost loading and product data entry.
What lives here: WC Products, WC Categories/Tags/Attributes, gallery-slider-for-woocommerce, product-video-gallery-slider, filter-everything, ACF product fields.
06
The Website
Build and maintain the website
Pages, posts, page builder, banners, sidebars, media library. Absorbs the old "Media & Assets" area.
What lives here: WP Posts/Pages, Elementor + Pro, ACF Pro, duplicate-page, woosidebars, dcj-global-banners, media library, alt-text tooling.
07
Marketing & Analytics
Run marketing
Promotional campaigns, GA tracking, sales analytics, marketing email, reports.
What lives here: WC Marketing, woocommerce-google-analytics-integration, WC Reports + Analytics, wp-mail-smtp (when used for marketing email), add-email-signature.
08
Site Operations
Keep the site running
WP/site settings, themes, email/SMTP, branding, plugin status, tax configuration.
What lives here: WP Settings, WP Appearance, WC Settings (general/advanced/tax), built2buy-helper settings, wp-mail-smtp config, admin-notices-manager.
09
Platform
Run the platform
Multi-tenant operations, dev tools, and integrations. Built2Buy super-admins only — never visible to customers.
What lives here: Network Admin, NS Cloner, Query Monitor, Redis Cache, ManageWP Worker, woo-update-manager, integration credentials (QB/Webgility, UPS API, EDI, marketplaces).
Platform-only
10
Returns & Tickets
Handle returns and customer issues
RMA workflow, restocking fees, condition grading, vendor chargebacks, customer-service tickets. Currently scattered; this is the new home.
What lives here: Today: nothing formal — handled via order edits and email. After redesign: a dedicated returns workflow + ticket queue.
Newly activated

Footnotes — where cells need extra detail

  1. Owner pricing — full authority. Owner sets list prices, margin policy, pricing tiers. Sales Director and Regional Sales Manager negotiate within Owner-set boundaries.
  2. Sales Director pricing — customer-specific contracts. Sets account-level pricing within Owner-set policy. Cannot change list prices or margin floors without Owner approval.
  3. Regional scope. Sees and acts on accounts within their assigned region only. Does not see other regions' orders, customers, or pricing.
  4. Account writes vs. reads. Phone Team and Order Processor can write contact notes, billing/shipping addresses, and order-related fields, but cannot create new accounts or change role assignments.
  5. Assigned-account scope. Field Sales Rep sees and acts on only their assigned accounts. Orders, accounts, and tickets all scoped to that list.
  6. Marketing — promotions only, not list pricing. Marketing Coordinator owns coupons, promo campaigns, marketing-driven discounts. Read-only on list/wholesale prices. The "Pricing & Promotions" area may need to split into two for the engineering brief.
  7. Catalog Manager — cost only, not sell price. Loads vendor cost on products, but cannot edit sell price. Sell-price authority belongs to Owner / Sales Director, not the buyer/catalog person.
  8. Bookkeeper on returns. Read-only on the financial side of returns (refund amounts, credit memos). Does not approve or process returns themselves — that's Order Processor and Shipping & Warehouse Lead.

Open questions to resolve before this matrix locks

  • Should the "Pricing & Promotions" area split into two (List Pricing + Promotions) so Marketing's promo-only authority becomes a clean cell?
  • Is "Regional Sales Manager region scope" something every customer configures, or only mid+ customers? (Affects whether the role exists on small-customer instances at all.)
  • Does Sales Director have any access to The Website (read-only on what's published) or none?
  • Should Shipping & Warehouse Lead have any access to Pricing & Promotions (e.g., shipping-cost components surface in pricing logic)?
  • External Bookkeeper (accounting firm) login — does it inherit the same matrix row, or get a stricter read-only-everywhere variant?
